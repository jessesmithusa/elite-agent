### Moment

At the Tokyo Olympics team final, Simone Biles lost air awareness ("the twisties") during a vault and had to decide whether to keep competing. She withdrew to protect her safety, supported her teammates from the sidelines, and later returned to win bronze on beam.

### What Usually Happens

Pressure from the crowd, team, and self-expectation pushes toward continuing despite changed conditions.

### Premise

When your situation changes, the safe choice changes too. You cannot control what the crowd expects. You can control your decision based on present facts.

### Desired Outcome

Protect physical safety and mental health by stopping when the risk becomes real.

### Controllable Behavior

When conditions change, pause and re-decide based on what is true now, not what was planned before.

### Review Question

Did you pause to re-evaluate the situation before committing to your next action?

### Athlete Level

High school, college, pro, Olympian.

### Notes For Younger Athletes

Talk to a coach or parent if you feel unsafe or unable to perform safely, even if you had planned to compete.

### Key Insight

The disciplined decision was stopping, not pushing through.
