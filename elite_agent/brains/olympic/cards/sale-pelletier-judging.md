### Moment

Canadian pairs skaters Jamie Salé and David Pelletier skated a clean free program at Salt Lake City 2002 but were placed second in a judging decision that immediately drew controversy. A French judge said she had been pressured; after an investigation, the IOC awarded them a duplicate gold.

### What Usually Happens

Immediately attacking the other skaters or officials in response to the unfair call.

### Premise

You cannot control judging. You cannot control whether a call is fair. You can control your response and how you represent yourself.

### Desired Outcome

Stay composed and speak only about your own performance while the process works itself out.

### Controllable Behavior

When a call goes against you, respond about your own performance, not the officials or other competitors.

### Review Question

Did you focus on your own performance and avoid attacking others?

### Athlete Level

High school, college, pro, Olympian.

### Notes For Younger Athletes

If you feel a call was unfair, talk to your coach. Let them help you understand what to do next.

### Key Insight

They couldn't control the judging, only their response — and their composure was vindicated.
