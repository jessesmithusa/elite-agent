### Moment

Andre Agassi, later the 1996 Olympic gold medalist, wrote in his autobiography that he hated tennis for long stretches of his career. After his ranking fell to No. 141 in 1997, he rebuilt through low-level Challenger tournaments and daily conditioning with trainer Gil Reyes, returning to No. 1 in 1999.

### What Usually Happens

Waiting for motivation to return before committing to training or competing.

### Premise

Motivation often comes after action, not before. You cannot control how you feel. You can control what you do today.

### Desired Outcome

Return to competitive form by committing to small, unglamorous daily steps.

### Controllable Behavior

When avoiding, shrink the task to today's single unglamorous rep and do it regardless of mood.

### Review Question

Did you complete today's small commitment even though you did not feel like it?

### Athlete Level

High school, college, pro, Olympian.

### Notes For Younger Athletes

If you feel stuck or unmotivated for a long time, talk to a coach, parent, or counselor.

### Key Insight

Act your way into commitment instead of waiting for the feeling.

### For Coach

"What is the smallest daily commitment your athlete can make? How do you celebrate small steps?"
