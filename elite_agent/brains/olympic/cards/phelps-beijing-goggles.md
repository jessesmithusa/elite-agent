### Moment

Michael Phelps's goggles leaked and filled with water during the 200m butterfly final at the Beijing 2008 Olympics. He swam most of the race unable to see, navigated by the stroke counts he had rehearsed in practice, and won gold with a world record.

### What Usually Happens

A swimmer loses visual reference, panics, and cannot navigate the race course.

### Premise

Equipment can fail without warning. You cannot control equipment failure, but you can prepare for it.

### Desired Outcome

Complete the race accurately despite losing visual guidance.

### Controllable Behavior

Count strokes per lap in training so that if vision fails, you can navigate by counting.

### Review Question

Can you swim your race by stroke count alone if you lose visual reference?

### Athlete Level

High school, college, pro, Olympian.

### Notes For Younger Athletes

Talk to your coach about what to do if equipment fails during a race.

### Key Insight

Rehearse your response to likely failures before they happen.

### For Coach

"Have your swimmers practice navigating by stroke count. What equipment failures should we prepare for in your sport?"
