# Athlete Behavior Example

Use this template to contribute a real athlete moment. Fill in the short tag
block at the top (it's just a few one-line answers, not real YAML skills
required) so the routing system can find your story, then write the story
itself in plain prose below — no code or formatting knowledge needed.

Do not include private athlete names, emails, phone numbers, medical details, payment details, recruiting details, or anything you would not want public.

## Tags (fill in the blanks)

Copy the block below, keep the `---` lines, and replace each value. These
lines are read by the card lookup system, so keep each answer short (a few
words) and keep the quotes.

```yaml
---
sport: "basketball"          # the sport or activity, e.g. basketball, water polo, soccer, swimming
skill: "staying composed after a bad call"   # the specific skill or pattern this story teaches, in a few words
situation_type: "conflict"   # pick ONE: avoidance | overwhelm | decisiveness | conflict | general
big5_facets: "neuroticism:high, conscientiousness:low"   # optional: the personality traits this story fits (see guide below)
---
```

- `sport` — the sport or activity the story happened in.
- `skill` — the specific behavior or pattern the story is teaching, in a few words.
- `situation_type` — the single best-fit category for this moment. Choose one: `avoidance` (putting things off), `overwhelm` (too much at once), `decisiveness` (needing to choose or act fast), `conflict` (disagreement with a ref, teammate, opponent, or coach), or `general` (doesn't fit the others).
- `big5_facets` — optional. If you know the athlete tends toward certain traits (for example, gets anxious easily, or is very disciplined), note it here as `trait:level` pairs. Leave the default or omit the line if you're not sure — this is optional and a coach can fill it in later.

## What Usually Happens

## Premise

What is true about this moment, and what is outside the athlete's control?

Example: The referee may make bad calls. I cannot control the call. I can control my next action.

## Desired Outcome

What does the athlete want, and why does this behavior help?

Example: I want to win and not get distracted by arguing with the referee.

## Controllable Behavior

Example: When I disagree with a call, I will turn away, take one breath, and sprint back on defense.

## Review Question

What yes/no/almost question proves whether the behavior happened?

Example: Did I turn away, breathe once, and sprint back on defense after calls I disagreed with?

### Sport

Basketball

### Moment

### Premise

The referee may make bad calls. I cannot control the call. I can control my next action.

### Desired Outcome

I want to win and not get distracted by arguing with the referee.

### Controllable Behavior

When I disagree with a call, I will turn away, take one breath, and sprint back on defense.

Did I turn away, breathe once, and sprint back on defense after calls I disagreed with?

### Athlete Level

Youth through college.

### Notes For Younger Athletes

Escalate threats, discriminatory language, injury, abuse, or unsafe conduct to a trusted adult or qualified professional.

### Key Insight

Turning the moment into a fixed sequence (turn, breathe, sprint) removes the decision from the heat of the moment.
